# Key Findings

## Sales and Customers
- The dataset contains a sample of marketplace transactions and customers.
- Customer demand is concentrated in a few states, especially São Paulo (SP).
- Average order value varies across states, suggesting differences in customer spending behavior.

## Sellers
- Revenue is distributed across multiple sellers, but performance varies significantly.
- Some sellers generate high revenue through volume, while others do so through higher-value transactions.
- Seller geography is heavily concentrated in São Paulo (SP), which dominates total seller orders and revenue.

## Products
- Product demand is spread across multiple categories.
- `cama_mesa_banho` is one of the strongest revenue-driving categories.
- Some categories rely on high volume and lower prices, while others generate revenue through premium pricing.

## Delivery
- Around 8% of delivered orders are late.
- State-level delivery performance among major states ranges from roughly 4% to 10%.
- Seller-level delivery analysis reveals much larger variation than state-level analysis.
- Some sellers maintain 0% late delivery rates over meaningful order volumes, while others exceed 25% to 35%.

## Root Cause Interpretation
- Delivery delays appear to be driven more by individual seller execution than by broad regional logistics issues alone.
- Top-performing sellers demonstrate that low delay rates are achievable even at higher order volume.
